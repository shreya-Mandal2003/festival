FESTIVALS OF BENGAL

A festival is an event ordinarily celebrated by a community and centering on some characteristic aspect of that community and its religion or cultures.

DURGA PUJA

Durga Puja, also known as Durgotsava, or Sharodotsava, is an annual Hindu festival originating in the Indian subcontinent which reveres and pays homage to the Hindu goddess, Durga.

BHAI DOOj

Bhai Dooj,Bhai Phonta is a festival celebrated by Hindus on the second lunar day of Shukla Paksha in the Vikram Samvat Hindu calendar or of Shalivahan Shaka calendar month of Kartika.

HOLI

Holi is a popular ancient Indian festival, also known as the "Festival of Love", the "Festival of Colours", and the "Festival of Spring". The festival celebrates the eternal and divine love of Radha Krishna.

LAXMI PUJA

Lakshmi Puja is a Hindu religious festival that falls on Amavasya of Krishna Paksha in the Vikram Samvat Hindu calendar month of Kartik, on the third day of Deepawali.

More..

RAKHI BANDHAN

Raksha Bandhan, is a popular, traditionally Hindu,ceremony, which is central to a festival of the same name, celebrated in South Asia, and among people around the world influenced by Hindu culture.
